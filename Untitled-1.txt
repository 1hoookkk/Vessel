Master Spec: E-mu H-Chip (FX8010) Emulation

Objective: Create a component-level emulation of the Audity 2000 Z-Plane filter, prioritizing "Thinness," "Grit," and "Inter-stage Saturation" over audio fidelity.
1. Topology & Structure

    Architecture: Series Cascade (Chain).

    Order: 14-pole total, broken into 7 distinct Biquad Sections.

    Biquad Type: Transposed Direct Form II (TDF-II).

        Reasoning: Research 2 confirms TDF-II is the standard for fixed-point hardware to manage accumulator noise. Do not use Direct Form I.

    Filter Types: The engine must support Full Biquads (Poles + Zeros).

        Vowel Configuration: 1x LowPass/HighPass section + 6x Peaking/Resonator sections.

2. Fixed-Point Math Rules (The "Thin" Source)

    Data Path: 32-bit Integer (Q31 Format).

        Constraint: Audio samples must be converted to int32 at the input and back to float only at the very final output.

    Coefficients: 16-bit Integer (Q15 Format).

        Constraint: Floating-point coefficients calculated from the morphing logic must be truncated to 16-bit before being applied to the filter.

        Why: This quantization error causes the "thin" spectral smearing described in the research.
